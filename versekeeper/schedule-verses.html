Scheduling with VerseKeeper

Use VerseKeeper’s Schedule Verses (Beta) to create simple or custom schedules, and you will never forget to practice or review your scriptures.

Pastor and author John Piper at Desiring God says, “Average folks like me have to work real hard to memorize the first time, and then recurrently review to keep it.” That has also been true for me. Why spend all that time memorizing scripture and then forget it over time? Isn’t it better to come up with a schedule to review your memorized verses? Yup, I also thought it was better. That’s why I added the Schedule Verses feature.

You can create custom schedules for a scripture passage or a group of scripture by using labels. After you create a schedule, you can view all of your schedules in a list or using Schedule Verses’ day and month views.

Creating Schedules

Click the “+” icon to create your schedule. Choose the time of day and start date you would like to be reminded to review your scripture. 

The repeat section gives you the option of creating a recurring schedule. You have a few default choices:

  • Day: Monday through Sunday
  • Week: Once a week on the date you selected
  • Weekday: Monday through Friday
  • Weekend: Saturday & Sunday
  • Month: Once a month on the date you selected
  • Year: Once a year on the date you selected

However, I wanted more custom options as well. Tap “Custom…” to see the following scheduling options:

  • Repeat Every: Create a custom schedule that recurs based on your selection criteria.
    • Day: Create a recurring schedule for every nth day you selected.
    • Week: The schedule recurs every nth selected days of the week.
    • Month: The schedule recurs every nth selected day of the month or day of the week in the month.
    • Year: Create a recurring schedule for every nth selected year.
  • Repeat Ends: Determine when the schedule ends.
    • Never: The schedule will recur indefinitely.
    • On: The schedule will stop recurring on the selected date.
    • After: The schedule will stop after a selected number of occurrences.

Scripture or Labels:

VerseKeeper allows you to create a schedule based on a scriptures that you select.

However, you can also create schedules based on labels. Let’s say you want to create a schedule for the three chapters of Titus. Easy, just follow these simple steps:

  1. Assign Labels to the Scripture:
    • Option 1: Add labels to new scripture
      1. Add the three chapters of Titus individually using VerseKeeper’s Add Verses.
      2. While adding each chapter, create and assign a label to it called “Titus,” or whatever name you would like.
    • Option 2: Update labels on existing scripture
      1. Change the labels using VerseKeeper’s All Verses.
  2. Assign Labels to Schedules:
    • Choose which labels you want to assign to the schedule.

Notes & Color:

VerseKeeper allows you to add notes to your schedules. You can also choose a color for your schedule, which is reflected in the Schedule Verses’ list view, day view, and month view to easily distinguish between schedules.

Viewing Schedules

VerseKeeper provides three ways to view your schedules, so you will always know your scripture memory plan. Whether you like to view your schedules in a list view, day view, or month view, VerseKeeper has taken care of it.

Switching Views

You can change the schedule view by tapping the more icon on the top navigation menu or change views by tapping the more icon.

List View

List View is the default view for schedules in VerseKeeper. It is also the home screen for the Schedule Verses section. The next upcoming schedule is displayed at the top of the screen.

The rest of the screen displays the schedules that you have created. Each schedule displays the following:

  • Color-Coded Circle: Inside the circle is the schedule’s user-selected time. The circle can also be color-coded according to your preference. 
  • Schedule Overview: The schedule also shows your selected scripture or labels, the next alarm date, and how often it repeats.
  • On/Off Status: Quickly turn schedules on or off with one tap. Please note that you can only turn on repeating schedules from here.

You can select schedules in one of three ways:

  • Single Select: Use this option for making changes to one schedule.
    • Tap anywhere on the schedule overview
  • Multi-Select: This option is great for deleting or turning multiple schedules on or off simultaneously.
    • Tap the color-coded circle
    • Long press on the schedule overview

Day View:

The day view allows you to see schedules for a selected day by using the following gestures:

  • Swipe left or right to switch the currently displayed day. 
  • Swipe up or down to show more hours of the day. 
  • Tap the “Today” icon to go back to the current day. 
  • Tap a schedule and then tap “Open” to view or make changes to it. 

Month View:

The month view allows you to see a big-picture overview of the schedules for a selected month. 

The month view supports the following gestures:

  • Swipe left or right to switch the currently displayed month. 
  • Tap the “Today” icon to go back to the current month. 
  • Long pressing on a day gives you the option of opening it in the day view.

The month view has two options:

  • Sunday through Saturday: This option displays the whole month.
  • Monday through Friday: This option is perfect if you only memorize scripture during the weekday.

Note: To toggle between the two views, tap the “more” icon in the navigation menu and then tap “Mon. – Fri. Only.”
