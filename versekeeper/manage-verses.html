Easily Manage Your Verses

Manage your verses with ease in VerseKeeper. In the All Verses section, you can quickly identify a verse’s assigned labels, schedule status, and current category.

What are the labels assigned to a verse?

Quickly find a verse’s labels in the bottom right corner. However, a verse with multiple labels will not show them all. So, tap a verse to see all of its labels. See “Verse Detail” for more information.

What is a verse’s schedule status?

Verses can be in one of three schedule states:

  • Schedule Turned On: Verses that are part of an active schedule will have a turquoise-colored alarm icon.
  • Schedule Turned Off: Verses that are part of an inactive schedule will have a gray alarm icon.
  • No Schedule: Verses that do not have a schedule will not have an alarm icon at all.

Keep in mind that you can create schedules based on labels also. So, a verse can be a part of a schedule by the labels assigned to it. See Schedule Verses for more information.

What is a verse category:

VerseKeeper keeps things organized with categories. Verses can be in one of three categories:

  • Practice: The practice category is typically for verses that you haven’t memorized yet. The Practice Verses section of VerseKeeper will only show verses listed in the practice category.
  • Review: The review category is for verses that you have memorized, congrats! This category is for periodically testing the accuracy of your memory. The Review Verses section of VerseKeeper will only show verses listed in the review category.
  • Archive: Do you have a verse you don’t want to delete, but you are not currently practicing or reviewing it? Then archive it instead. Archived verses will not show up in the Practice Verses or Review Verses sections of VerseKeeper. By default, the All Verses section hides archived verses. However, the All Verses filters can show which verses are archived.

What about selecting verses?

VerseKeeper supports selecting multiple verses at a time to either change categories, add or remove labels, and delete verses. 

Select verses in one of three ways:

  • The Verse’s Circle: Tapping a verse’s turquoise circle selects it.
  • Tap & Hold: You can also tap and hold anywhere on a verse to select it.
  • Select All: Lastly, tapping the “select all” icon will select all the verses on the first tap. Tapping the “select all” icon again will unselect all the verses.

How do you sort & filter verses?

Are you a VerseKeeper power user? Then take advantage of the sorting and filtering feature. Speedily sort verses in ascending or descending order either by the Book of the Bible, date added, category, or Bible version.

Do you only want to see verses based on specific criteria? No problem! VerseKeeper allows you to create filters based on the following criteria:

  • Book of the Bible
  • Category
  • Label
  • Bible version
  • Schedule status

Filters can be cleared one at a time by tapping the “x” next to each criterion in the heading section. Or, reset filters by opening the filter menu and pressing “Okay” without making any changes.
